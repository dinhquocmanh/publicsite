Guide: How to Convert Between Decimal, Hex, and Binary
1. Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal

To convert decimal to hexadecimal, divide the decimal number by 16 repeatedly. Record the remainder after each division (this is your hexadecimal digit). The process stops when the quotient becomes 0. The hexadecimal number is the remainders read from bottom to top.

Example: Convert 255 to Hexadecimal:

  • 255 ÷ 16 = 15 remainder 15 (F in hex)
  • 15 ÷ 16 = 0 remainder 15 (F in hex)
  • Hexadecimal: FF
2. Convert Decimal to Binary

To convert decimal to binary, divide the decimal number by 2 repeatedly. Record the remainder after each division (this is your binary digit). The process stops when the quotient becomes 0. The binary number is the remainders read from bottom to top.

Example: Convert 10 to Binary:

  • 10 ÷ 2 = 5 remainder 0
  • 5 ÷ 2 = 2 remainder 1
  • 2 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 0
  • 1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1
  • Binary: 1010
